## Design Context

### Users
Small family — multiple people tracking shared and individual finances. Used at home in the evenings, primarily on desktop/laptop. The audience is financially aware and wants to see real data without being patronized by oversimplified UI.

### Brand Personality
**Rigorous. Shared. Transparent.**

This is a serious financial tool that a family actually relies on — not a lifestyle app. It should feel like something a disciplined person built for themselves: dense with information, zero tolerance for decoration that doesn't earn its place. Bloomberg Terminal meets Linear: confident data density, sharp hierarchy, no fluff.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Sharp & professional / terminal-editorial.**

- Dark default (dark evenings at home reviewing finances), with full light mode support
- Amber/gold accent — the colour of money, wealth, confidence. NOT the tired indigo/purple AI palette.
- OKLCH-based colour system with tinted dark neutrals (hue ~250, blue-slate)
- Condensed display typography for numbers and headings (Barlow Semi Condensed) — efficient, terminal-like
- Clean humanist body font (Epilogue) — warm enough for a family, professional enough for the data
- High information density: tight rows, small labels, big impactful numbers
- No decorative elements that don't carry data

### Design Principles
1. **Data density over decoration** — every pixel serves the data. No flourishes.
2. **Amber confidence** — singular accent colour, used sparingly and decisively.
3. **Condensed precision** — display font for all numbers and headings; narrow and efficient.
4. **Earned colour** — positive green, negative red, amber for brand. No other colours except category badges.
5. **Dark as default** — the product is used in dim home environments; dark theme should look exceptional, not like an afterthought.
